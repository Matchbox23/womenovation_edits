WHO WE ARE

OVERVIEW

The Womenovation Foundation is a non-profit organization utilizing local innovative talents in elevating the status of women. The organization’s primary aim is to invest in supporting design-based industries in generating economic opportunities for women. Our second organizational aim is to strengthen the culture of gender equality across society.

Given the low participation of women in the economy, effectively supporting design-based industries and providing women with entrepreneurship opportunities and decent jobs translate to economic growth and an accelerated progress in the achieving sustainable development goals (SDGs). Raising awareness on the importance of women’s equal rights and their ability to equally perform and deliver is a crucial factor to ensure all citizens become proactive in reducing gender disparities.

Azza Fahmy

Azza Fahmy

Trustee, Womenovation Foundation


Azza Fahmy is a pioneer in Egyptian design, known for her innovative and successful work. With a career spanning decades, Fahmy has become an influential figure in her field and a representative of Egyptian culture and art. Born and raised in Sohag, Upper Egypt, Fahmy earned her Bachelor of Arts in Interior Design from Cairo University before embarking on a career in jewelry making. As one of the first female apprentices in Cairo's Khan El Khalili district, Fahmy soon established her own workshop and rapidly gained international fame.

Fahmy's designs are rooted in Egyptian culture and heritage, which she often highlights in her work. Her Houses of the Nile collection was an early example of her efforts to highlight often-overlooked aspects of Egyptian life. Fahmy's calligraphy pieces, featuring Arabic poems and proverbs, have become an iconic trend in the region and inspired many others.

Fahmy has expanded her reach beyond jewelry, collaborating with fashion designers such as Julien Macdonald, Justin Thornton, and Thea Bregazzi. She has also partnered with cultural institutions, such as the British Museum, to create custom collections. In 2013, Fahmy established The Design Studio by Azza Fahmy in partnership with the Alchimia Contemporary Jewelry School in Florence. The studio, the first of its kind in Egypt and the Middle East, serves as a jewelry vocational school and positions Egypt as an international hub for jewelry design.

Fahmy's passion for her work is evident in her designs, which reflect her pride in Egyptian culture and heritage. She has used her experience and success to promote young and aspiring jewelers and to share her love of her country and its traditions with the world.

OUR STORY

The Spark

While analyzing data about women, Walid Osman, Womenovation’s Founder, noticed that 75% of Egyptian women eligible to work are inactive in the economy and concluded that these women are either working informally or living as dependents. His childhood interest in art and design sparked the idea of engaging those women in elevating the design industry while elevating their economic status.

Concept Development

After a period of research and drafting ideas, Osman developed an initial concept note titled “The Womenovation Initiative,” which he designed as a set of activities aiming to build the capacities of women with artistic talents and provide them with opportunities in the most relevant industries. Over the past few years, the National Council for Women (NCW) has achieved significant progress in the framework of women's empowerment. Consequently, the concept note was shared with NCW, the national institution responsible for ensuring women and men are equal in terms of their economic, political, and legislative rights.

Going Public

By overseeing the opportunity in the initial concept note, NCW supported the concept suggesting its execution in an institutionalized approach operating independently under a civil society framework. Moreover, NCW believed in the importance of recognizing the efforts of the concept developer, which led to Womenovation’s evolution into a Foundation. On Gender Day of COP 27, Womenovation’s Founder Walid Osman publicly presented an overview of Womenovation’s institutional framework and scope for the first time.

Launch

On 18 March 2023, the Womenovation Foundation had its official launch event at Cairo’s downtown gathering a diversity of audiences including artists, government officials, academics, diplomats, civil society organizations, private sector companies, students, and media professionals. In the event, Womenovation’s mission and scope were presented followed by short inspirational statements regarding the connection between development, art and design. Speakers included H.E. Nevine El-Kabbaj, Minister of Social Solidarity, Maya Morsy, President of Egypt’s National Council for Women, international jewelry designer Azza Famy, and prominent actress Shereen Reda. Talks were followed by an art exhibition and a display of products by emerging local designers.

The Beginning

A well-planned launch event presenting a clear mission and impactful scope of work was followed by connections with donors and stakeholders. Womenovation was able to grasp donor priorities, expand its network, and submit proposals to donors. In July 2023, Womenovation received its first funding approval from Alex Bank. The initial fund effective January 2024 is allocated to support Womenovation’s project “Promoting Innovation.”
